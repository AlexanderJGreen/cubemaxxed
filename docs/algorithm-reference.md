# CubeMaxxed — Algorithm Reference

## Purpose
This document contains the complete and accurate list of all 2-Look OLL and 2-Look PLL algorithms for the algorithms page. Claude Code should use this as the source of truth for algorithm names, notation, case descriptions, and cube state definitions.

## How to Use cubing.js for Case Visuals
The cubing.js library (https://cubing.js.org/) can render accurate 3D cube states. For each case below, a "setup" algorithm is provided. Applying the setup algorithm to a solved cube produces the exact cube state for that case. Use this to generate accurate visuals.

Install: npm install cubing
Docs: https://js.cubing.net/cubing/

## 2-Look OLL (10 cases total)

2-Look OLL solves the last layer orientation in two steps:
1. Orient edges first (make yellow cross) — 3 cases
2. Orient corners (complete yellow face) — 7 cases

### Step 1: Edge Orientation (EOLL) — 3 Cases

These cases assume the cross (white) is on the bottom. The goal is to get all 4 yellow edges facing up (forming a yellow cross on top).

#### EOLL 1 — Dot
- **Case description:** No yellow edges are oriented (facing up). You see a single yellow dot (the center) on top.
- **Recognition:** No yellow edges on top face, only the center.
- **Algorithm:** F (R U R' U') F' f (R U R' U') f'
- **Alternative:** F (R U R' U') F' — then check if you now have Line or L-shape, and apply the corresponding algorithm.
- **Setup (to create this state on a solved cube):** f U R U' R' f' F U R U' R' F'
- **Note:** This is OLL case #2 in full OLL numbering. Applying the Line algorithm twice or the L-shape algorithm from specific angles also works. The combined algorithm above solves it in one step.

#### EOLL 2 — L-Shape
- **Case description:** Two adjacent yellow edges are oriented. The two oriented edges form an "L" shape on the top face.
- **Recognition:** Two yellow edges on top forming an L. Hold the cube so the L is in the back-left corner (oriented edges point toward back and left).
- **Algorithm:** F (R U R' U') F'
- **Setup:** F U R U' R' F'
- **Note:** This is a subset of OLL cases #33-36 area. Hold the L so the two oriented edges are at the back and left (the L points to the back-left corner).

#### EOLL 3 — Line
- **Case description:** Two opposite yellow edges are oriented. They form a straight line across the top face.
- **Recognition:** Two yellow edges on top forming a straight horizontal line. Hold the line horizontal (left to right).
- **Algorithm:** F (R U R' U') F'
- **Setup:** F U R U' R' F' (from a different angle than the L-shape)
- **Note:** Hold the line horizontally (going left-right, not front-back) before executing. This is the same algorithm as the L-shape but the starting position differs.

### Step 2: Corner Orientation (OCLL) — 7 Cases

After making the yellow cross, these cases orient the 4 corners so the entire top face is yellow. The naming below uses common community names.

#### OCLL 1 — Sune (OLL 27)
- **Case name:** Sune
- **OLL number:** 27
- **Case description:** One corner is oriented (yellow facing up). The other three form a "fish" shape pointing to one direction. The oriented corner is at the front-left, and looking at the top, you see the fish shape.
- **Recognition:** One yellow corner on top. When holding the solved corner at front-left, the top face looks like a fish shape. Two yellow stickers visible on the front face, one on the right face.
- **Algorithm:** R U R' U R U2 R'
- **Setup:** R U2' R' U' R U' R'

#### OCLL 2 — Anti-Sune (OLL 26)
- **Case name:** Anti-Sune
- **OLL number:** 26
- **Case description:** One corner is oriented (yellow facing up). Mirror/inverse of Sune. The fish shape points the opposite direction.
- **Recognition:** One yellow corner on top. When holding the solved corner at front-right, the fish shape is mirrored compared to Sune. Two yellow stickers visible on the right face, one on the front face.
- **Algorithm:** R U2 R' U' R U' R'
- **Setup:** R U R' U R U2' R'
- **Note:** Some sources teach this as: R' U' R U' R' U2 R (same result, different angle)

#### OCLL 3 — H / Double Sune (OLL 21)
- **Case name:** H (or "Cross" or "Double Sune")
- **OLL number:** 21
- **Case description:** All four corners have yellow facing the front or back — no corners are oriented on top. Yellow stickers on the corners are all on the side faces, forming a specific pattern.
- **Recognition:** No yellow corners on top face. Looking at the front face, you see two yellow stickers in the top corners. Same on the back face. Left and right faces have no yellow corner stickers showing.
- **Algorithm:** R U R' U R U' R' U R U2 R'
- **Setup:** R U2' R' U' R U R' U' R U' R'

#### OCLL 4 — Pi / Bruno (OLL 22)
- **Case name:** Pi (or "Bruno")
- **OLL number:** 22
- **Case description:** No corners are oriented on top. Yellow stickers on the corners are visible on two adjacent side faces.
- **Recognition:** No yellow corners on top face. One side (e.g., the front) shows two yellow corner stickers. The opposite side (back) shows two yellow corner stickers. It looks like the pi (π) symbol from above.
- **Algorithm:** R U2 R2' U' R2 U' R2' U2 R
- **Setup:** R' U2' R2' U R2' U R2' U2' R'

#### OCLL 5 — Headlights (OLL 23)
- **Case name:** Headlights
- **OLL number:** 23
- **Case description:** Two corners are oriented on top (diagonally opposite). The other two have yellow facing the same side face, looking like car headlights.
- **Recognition:** Two yellow corners on top face (diagonal). One side face shows two yellow stickers side by side (the headlights). Hold the headlights facing you.
- **Algorithm:** R2 D R' U2 R D' R' U2 R'
- **Setup:** R U2' R D R' U2' R D' R2'

#### OCLL 6 — Chameleon / Bowtie (OLL 24)
- **Case name:** Chameleon (or "Bowtie")
- **OLL number:** 24
- **Case description:** Two adjacent corners are oriented on top. The other two have yellow facing different side faces.
- **Recognition:** Two yellow corners on top that are next to each other (adjacent, not diagonal). The two unsolved corners have yellow stickers pointing in different directions (one left, one front, for example).
- **Algorithm:** r U R' U' r' F R F'
- **Setup:** F R' F' r U R U' r'

#### OCLL 7 — Blinker / Triple Sune (OLL 25)
- **Case name:** Blinker (or "Triple Sune")
- **OLL number:** 25
- **Case description:** Two adjacent corners are oriented on top. Similar to Chameleon but the unsolved corners point differently.
- **Recognition:** Two yellow corners on top that are next to each other. Differs from Chameleon in the direction the unsolved corners' yellow stickers face. The unsolved corners' yellow stickers both face the same side.
- **Algorithm:** F' r U R' U' r' F R
- **Setup:** R' F' r U R U' r' F
- **Alternative:** R U2 R D R' U2 R D' R2 (all R and U moves)

## 2-Look PLL (6 cases total)

2-Look PLL solves the last layer permutation in two steps:
1. Permute corners first — 2 cases
2. Permute edges — 4 cases

### Step 1: Corner Permutation (CPLL) — 2 Cases

After OLL is complete (full yellow face), look at the side faces of the top layer corners. You're looking for "headlights" — two matching corner stickers on the same face.

#### CPLL 1 — T-Perm (Adjacent Corner Swap)
- **Case name:** T-Perm
- **Case description:** Two adjacent corners need to be swapped. One face will have matching corner colors (headlights), the other three will not.
- **Recognition:** Find the side with headlights (two matching corner stickers). Hold that side on the LEFT. Then execute the algorithm.
- **Algorithm:** R U R' U' R' F R2 U' R' U' R U R' F'
- **Setup:** F R' U' R U R U R' F' R U R' U' R' F R F'
- **Note:** The T-Perm is used here because it swaps two adjacent corners (and two edges, but we fix edges in step 2).

#### CPLL 2 — Y-Perm (Diagonal Corner Swap)
- **Case name:** Y-Perm
- **Case description:** Two diagonal corners need to be swapped. No face has matching corner colors (no headlights anywhere).
- **Recognition:** Look at all four sides. If NO side has matching corner colors (headlights), you have the diagonal swap case. Hold in any direction.
- **Algorithm:** F R U' R' U' R U R' F' R U R' U' R' F R F'
- **Setup:** F R' U' R U R U R' F' R U R' U' R' F R F' (from specific angle)
- **Note:** After executing, the corners will be solved but the edges will likely need permuting. Move to step 2.

---

### Step 2: Edge Permutation (EPLL) — 4 Cases

After corners are correctly placed, the edges need to be cycled into their correct positions.

#### EPLL 1 — Ua-Perm (Clockwise 3-cycle)
- **Case name:** Ua-Perm (U-perm a)
- **Case description:** Three edges need to cycle clockwise. One edge is already solved.
- **Recognition:** One side has a fully solved bar (all three top-layer stickers match). The other three sides each have one correct sticker and mismatches. The edges cycle clockwise relative to the solved edge.
- **Algorithm:** R U' R U R U R U' R' U' R2
- **Alternative:** M2 U M U2 M' U M2
- **Setup:** R2 U R U R' U' R' U' R' U R'
- **Note:** Hold the solved edge at the BACK.

#### EPLL 2 — Ub-Perm (Counter-clockwise 3-cycle)
- **Case name:** Ub-Perm (U-perm b)
- **Case description:** Three edges need to cycle counter-clockwise. One edge is already solved.
- **Recognition:** Same as Ua but the cycle goes the opposite direction. One solved bar, but the mismatches rotate the other way.
- **Algorithm:** R2 U R U R' U' R' U' R' U R'
- **Alternative:** M2 U' M U2 M' U' M2
- **Setup:** R U' R U R U R U' R' U' R2
- **Note:** Hold the solved edge at the BACK.

#### EPLL 3 — H-Perm (Opposite Edge Swap)
- **Case name:** H-Perm
- **Case description:** Two pairs of opposite edges need to be swapped. Front↔Back and Left↔Right.
- **Recognition:** Every side has the center sticker matching but the two edge-adjacent stickers on each side are swapped. It looks like every face has a checkerboard pattern on the top layer. No single solved bar anywhere.
- **Algorithm:** M2 U M2 U2 M2 U M2
- **Setup:** M2 U M2 U2 M2 U M2 (same algorithm — it's its own inverse)
- **Note:** Can be executed from any angle since it swaps opposite pairs.

#### EPLL 4 — Z-Perm (Adjacent Edge Swap)
- **Case name:** Z-Perm
- **Case description:** Two pairs of adjacent edges need to be swapped. Each pair swaps with its neighbor.
- **Recognition:** Similar to H-perm (no single solved bar) but the pattern is different. Two adjacent sides will have their edge stickers matching each other (swapped neighbors). Forms a Z or S pattern when viewed from above.
- **Algorithm:** M2 U M2 U M' U2 M2 U2 M' U2
- **Alternative:** M' U M2 U M2 U M' U2 M2
- **Setup:** Apply the algorithm itself (self-inverse with setup)
- **Note:** Hold the cube so that the two correctly-colored edge stickers on the front face are on the left and right sides.
