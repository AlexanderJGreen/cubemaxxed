# Speedcubing Platform — Curriculum Outline

## Overview
Takes a user from never having touched a cube to a full CFOP solve approaching sub-30.
43 lessons across 7 stages.

---

## Stage 1 — The Basics (5 lessons)
Goal: Understand how the cube works, read notation, do basic moves.

- **1.1 — What is a Rubik's Cube?**
  Teaches: Centers, edges, corners. Centers don't move. Goal is matching colors to centers. Quiz: identify piece types from images.

- **1.2 — How the Cube Moves**
  Teaches: The 6 faces (U, D, L, R, F, B). What a "turn" is. Clockwise vs counterclockwise. Quiz: "Which face is this?"

- **1.3 — Reading Notation**
  Teaches: R, R', R2, U, U', etc. What prime means. What double moves mean. Quiz: given a notation string like R U R', describe or execute the moves.

- **1.4 — Your First Algorithms**
  Teaches: What an algorithm is. Practice the sexy move (R U R' U'). See how repeating it returns the cube to solved state. Quiz: execute the sexy move X times.

- **1.5 — Holding the Cube**
  Teaches: Proper grip, which face should face you, basic hand positioning. Sets up good habits early.

---

## Stage 2 — Your First Solve (9 lessons)
Goal: Solve the entire cube using the beginner layer-by-layer method.

- **2.1 — The White Cross (Concept)**
  Teaches: Why we start with white. What a "correct" cross piece looks like (white + matching side color). Not just white on top — edges must align with centers.

- **2.2 — The White Cross (Solving)**
  Teaches: Getting the 4 white edge pieces into place. More intuitive than algorithmic. Cases: edge on bottom, middle, or top.

- **2.3 — White Corners**
  Teaches: Finding white corner pieces, inserting into correct slots. R U R' type insertions. Different cases based on corner location and white face orientation.

- **2.4 — Middle Layer Edges**
  Teaches: Flip cube over (white on bottom). Finding middle layer edge pieces. Two algorithms — insert right and insert left.

- **2.5 — Yellow Cross**
  Teaches: Getting a yellow cross on top. Three cases (dot, L-shape, line) and the algorithm to cycle through them.

- **2.6 — Yellow Edge Alignment**
  Teaches: Yellow cross side colors might not match centers. Algorithm to cycle edges until they do.

- **2.7 — Yellow Corner Positioning**
  Teaches: Getting yellow corners into the right spots (even if twisted wrong). Algorithm for cycling corners.

- **2.8 — Yellow Corner Orientation**
  Teaches: Twisting corners so yellow faces up. The R' D' R D technique. Careful explanation — this step looks like you're messing up the cube.

- **2.9 — Your First Full Solve**
  Teaches: Nothing new. Guided walkthrough of an entire solve. Big XP reward and "First Solve" achievement badge.

## Stage 3 — Getting Comfortable (6 lessons)
Goal: Turn the rough first solve into something smooth and consistent. Target: sub-2:00.

- **3.1 — Finger Tricks (Basics)**
  Teaches: Using fingers instead of wrist turns. Right index for U, right thumb for R, left index for U'.

- **3.2 — Finger Tricks (Practice)**
  Teaches: Apply finger tricks to known algorithms. Drill the sexy move with proper technique. Timed challenge opportunity.

- **3.3 — Efficient Cross**
  Teaches: Planning white cross before turning. Solve cross in 8 moves or fewer. Introduce solving cross on bottom (white down).

- **3.4 — Reducing Pauses**
  Teaches: Basic lookahead. While finishing one step, start looking for the next piece. Less staring = faster solves.

- **3.5 — Common Mistakes & Bad Habits**
  Teaches: Rotating the whole cube too much, always solving cross in same order, not using both hands, constant re-gripping.

- **3.6 — Benchmark Challenge**
  Teaches: Nothing new. Do 5 timed solves, get baseline average. Achievement unlock. Sets up profile stats.

## Stage 4 — Intro to CFOP & The Cross (5 lessons)
Goal: Understand CFOP and master the cross at a higher level.

- **4.1 — What is CFOP?**
  Teaches: The four steps — Cross, F2L, OLL, PLL. How it differs from beginner method. Briefly mention other methods exist (Roux, ZZ) but emphasize CFOP is the most popular route and the way to go for now.

- **4.2 — Cross on Bottom**
  Teaches: Solving cross with white on bottom from the start. Why this matters — immediate F2L transition without flipping.

- **4.3 — Planning the Full Cross**
  Teaches: Using inspection time to plan all 4 cross pieces before turning. Start with planning 2, work up to 4.

- **4.4 — Efficient Cross Solutions**
  Teaches: Solving cross in under 6 moves. Recognizing multiple solutions and picking the shortest.

- **4.5 — Cross Drills**
  Teaches: Timed cross-only solves. Goal: consistent cross in 2-3 seconds. Challenge and achievement opportunity.

---

## Stage 5 — F2L / First Two Layers (7 lessons)
Goal: Learn intuitive F2L. Hardest stage — emphasis on understanding over memorization.

- **5.1 — What is F2L?**
  Teaches: Pairing a corner + matching edge and inserting together instead of separately. Side-by-side comparison with beginner method.

- **5.2 — Pairs and Slots**
  Teaches: What a "pair" is, what a "slot" is. How to identify which corner goes with which edge. 4 pairs, 4 slots. Quiz: identify pairs.

- **5.3 — Basic Insertions (Easy Cases)**
  Teaches: Corner and edge already paired on top — just insert. R U R' (right slot) and L' U' L (left slot).

- **5.4 — Pairing on Top**
  Teaches: Both pieces on top but not paired. U moves to align, setup move to connect. Intuition building.

- **5.5 — Dealing with Pieces in the Wrong Slot**
  Teaches: Pulling pieces out of wrong slots without messing up solved pairs, then solving normally.

- **5.6 — Tricky Cases**
  Teaches: Harder situations — weird corner orientations, flipped edges. Thinking process: get both pieces to top, set up, pair, insert.

- **5.7 — F2L Practice & Building Intuition**
  Teaches: Full solves focusing on F2L quality, not speed. Achievement for first full CFOP-style F2L.

---

## Stage 6 — 2-Look OLL (5 lessons)
Goal: Orient the last layer in two steps using ~9 algorithms.

- **6.1 — What is OLL?**
  Teaches: Goal is making entire top face yellow. Full OLL = 57 algorithms, 2-look = ~9. Much more approachable.

- **6.2 — Step 1: Edge Orientation (Yellow Cross)**
  Teaches: Get yellow cross on top. 3 cases — dot, L-shape, line. Familiar from Stage 2.5, now with better finger tricks.

- **6.3 — Step 2: Corner Orientation (Complete Yellow Face)**
  Teaches: Orient corners so full face is yellow. 7 cases. Algorithms introduced one at a time with recognition tips.

- **6.4 — OLL Recognition Practice**
  Teaches: Fast recognition drills. Given a top-face state, identify the case and pick the right algorithm. Great for spaced repetition.

- **6.5 — Putting OLL Together**
  Teaches: Full practice. Cross + F2L + 2-look OLL. Focus on smooth transitions between OLL steps.

---

## Stage 7 — 2-Look PLL (6 lessons)
Goal: Permute the last layer in two steps using 6 algorithms. Complete CFOP. Approach sub-30.

- **7.1 — What is PLL?**
  Teaches: Move top layer pieces into correct positions. Full PLL = 21 algorithms, 2-look = 6.

- **7.2 — Step 1: Corner Permutation**
  Teaches: Get corners into right spots. 2 cases — one correct corner (cycle other three) or none correct (run algorithm twice).

- **7.3 — Step 2: Edge Permutation**
  Teaches: Cycle edges into position. 4 cases — U-perm (clockwise), U-perm (counterclockwise), Z-perm, H-perm. Recognition tips.

- **7.4 — PLL Recognition Practice**
  Teaches: Identify cases quickly by reading side colors. Spaced repetition / quiz opportunity.

- **7.5 — Full CFOP Solve**
  Teaches: Guided full CFOP solve — cross, F2L, 2-look OLL, 2-look PLL. Timed solves. "CFOP Complete" achievement badge.

- **7.6 — The Path to Sub-30**
  Teaches: What separates 45s from sub-30. Tips: cross efficiency, F2L lookahead, turning speed, reducing pauses. Practice routine for the playground. Launchpad into self-driven improvement.
